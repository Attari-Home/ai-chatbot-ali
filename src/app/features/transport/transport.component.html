Transportation in UAE

Discover various transportation options available in the UAE for a smooth and convenient travel experience.

Transportation Options

  • Taxis: Widely available throughout the UAE with easy hailing options.
  • Ride-hailing Services: Apps like Uber and Careem for convenient rides.
  • Public Transport: Buses and metro systems in major cities like Dubai and Abu Dhabi.
  • Rental Cars: International car rental companies available at airports and major locations.
  • Water Taxis: Available in Dubai for transport across the waterways.

Emergency Numbers

In case of emergencies while traveling, use these important contact numbers:

999 Police & General Emergency
998 Ambulance & Medical Emergency
997 Fire Services

Always keep these numbers handy and call immediately in case of any emergency situation.

What to Do in an Emergency

  • Road Accident: Stay calm, move to safety if possible, call 999 immediately, and provide your location.
  • Medical Emergency: Call 998 for ambulance. If you speak Arabic, say "Imshee" (go) or provide your location in English.
  • Fire Emergency: Call 997. Evacuate the area and follow instructions from emergency personnel.
  • Crime or Threat: Call 999 for police assistance. Stay in a safe location if possible.

Additional Emergency Contacts

RTA (Roads & Transport Authority): 800-6987 (Dubai)

Dubai Police: 999 (non-emergency: 901)

Abu Dhabi Police: 999 (non-emergency: 02-410-0000)

Sharjah Police: 999 (non-emergency: 065-333-333)

Coast Guard: 996 (for maritime emergencies)

Travel Tips

  • Download ride-hailing apps for easy transportation booking.
  • Check traffic conditions using apps like Dubai Traffic or Abu Dhabi Traffic.
  • Have local currency or card ready for taxis and rides.
  • Learn basic Arabic phrases for better communication.
  • Stay hydrated and wear appropriate clothing for the weather.
